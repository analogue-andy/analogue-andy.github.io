Hi there, I’m Andy, a performance-conscious front-end developer from Leeds, UK.

A Little About Me

In 2015, after several successful years slinging fancy cocktails to the good folk of Leeds, a fortuitous opportunity arose to turn a long-time hobby into my vocation, and it has been full steam ahead ever since. I cut my teeth on over 30 projects, large and small, working alongside and learning from some very clever people. For a bartender-turned-developer, my working day has drastically changed, but the values, principles, keen eye for detail, and passion for doing things properly that served me so well in the past are still driving me today.

Coding Methodologies and Principles

I like my code as I like my home: clean, tidy, and in order. I adopt and combine several methodologies to create scalable, modular UIs. I write semantic HTML and use a CSS class naming convention based on the BEM methodology and adapted by Harry Roberts. I take a leaf from Nicole Sullivan’s book by applying OOCSS principles.

  • OOCSS
  • BEMIT (BEM + ITCSS)
  • Atomic Design
  • DRY
  • Single Responsibility Principle
  • Separation of Concerns

Best Practices

The web is for everyone, and I want to ensure that the work I produce is as inclusive and accessible as possible. But it is about more than just disabilities. The responsibility is in providing help to all users who face any barriers to using the web. This includes catering for those accessing the web using assistive technologies or on poor networks. The goal is to ensure that everyone can enjoy a positive web experience and get to the information they need. Coding for accessibility is a learning process and I am a fledgling student, but the first step is in being mindful of it.
